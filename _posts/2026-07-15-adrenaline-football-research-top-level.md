I was so excited before the #FWC2026 match #Norge-#Brasil. The Norwegian team did have very strong adrenaline and had a convincing win over the five-time champions Brazil.

![Before the Norway vs Brazil match in FIFA World Cup 2026](/images/posts/739165195_10164420247192173_1406474919808982892_n.jpg)

I had very strong adrenaline in highly competitive mood when I was in Brazil in April, participating in two great conferences, XP 2026 and ICSE 2026, while leading a big HE project proposal as a coordinator and a main PI, competing for 19MEUR in a race of 2% success rate.

In #saopaulo, I co-organised and co-chaired a successful workshop AI4TD with 30+ participants, many from industry. I also gave a presentation at XP in Sao Paulo, had a 4am online meeting with partners in Europe, then joined the morning run at 6am with other international participants of XP in the beautiful Ibirapuera Park.

In #riodejaneiro, I gave an industry talk, networked with many at the big conference, and worked hard on the proposal with key partners who also attended the conference. The only social event I managed there was the Fla-Flu derby at the iconic #Maracana stadium on a beautiful Sunday afternoon with other conference participants.

![At Maracana during the Fla-Flu derby in Rio de Janeiro](/images/posts/740425576_10164420246627173_7254686238449048452_n.jpg)

We did manage to submit a strong proposal with the deadline exactly in the middle of ICSE.

The adrenaline run of the Norwegian team at this year FIFA World Cup 2026 ended at the quarter final, but that is life. They were received as national heroes coming back to Norway.

As researchers, whether we win funding in a race with a 2% success rate or not, we still compete at the highest level.
